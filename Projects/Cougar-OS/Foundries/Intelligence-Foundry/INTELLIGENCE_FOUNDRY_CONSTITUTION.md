# Preamble

The Intelligence Foundry‚ĄĘ exists to govern how Cougar OS discovers, verifies, preserves, organizes, and distributes knowledge from the external world.

Its purpose is not to collect the most information.

Its purpose is to create the most trustworthy, relevant, explainable, and organizationally useful intelligence possible.

The Intelligence Foundry operates under the Cougar Constitution‚ĄĘ and may not bypass the platform's requirements for governance, provenance, verification, security, organizational ownership, or human authority.

---

# Article I ‚ÄĒ Foundry Purpose

The Intelligence Foundry is responsible for the full lifecycle of external intelligence.

Its responsibilities include:

- source governance
- collection authorization
- collection scheduling
- collection-agent management
- mission creation
- verification
- provenance preservation
- Intelligence DNA‚ĄĘ creation
- organizational-impact evaluation
- graph and memory routing
- correction handling
- executive escalation
- retirement of outdated intelligence

The Foundry transforms external information into governed organizational intelligence.

---

# Article II ‚ÄĒ Intelligence Before Automation

No external observation may directly trigger permanent organizational action without evaluation.

External information must pass through the governed intelligence lifecycle before it may influence:

- the Enterprise Organization Graph‚ĄĘ
- Memory Foundry‚ĄĘ
- Executive Lanes‚ĄĘ
- missions
- decisions
- recommendations
- risk alerts
- autonomous workflows

Urgent intelligence may be escalated quickly, but it may not bypass provenance, attribution, and verification requirements.

---

# Article III ‚ÄĒ Governed Source Registry

No website, API, RSS feed, database, document stream, news organization, research source, government system, or internal feed may be used until it is registered in the Intelligence Source Registry.

Every registered source must include:

- source identity
- source type
- ownership
- approved access method
- assigned Lane
- authorized agents
- credibility classification
- licensing classification
- review status
- operational status
- collection constraints
- correction policy
- retirement status

Unregistered sources are prohibited from autonomous collection.

---

# Article IV ‚ÄĒ Source Approval

Every source must be reviewed before activation.

Source approval must consider:

- authority
- reliability
- update frequency
- historical accuracy
- transparency
- licensing
- accessibility
- relevance
- security risk
- manipulation risk
- operational cost
- organizational value

Approval is specific to a use case.

A source approved for research may not automatically be approved for trading, security, executive action, or customer-facing recommendations.

---

# Article V ‚ÄĒ Agent Authorization

Every collection action must be attributable to an authorized Collection Agent.

Each Collection Agent must have:

- a permanent agent identity
- an owning Lane
- an authorized source list
- approved collection methods
- assigned capabilities
- operating limits
- escalation rules
- retry rules
- audit history

Agents may not collect from sources outside their approved scope.

---

# Article VI ‚ÄĒ Mission-Based Collection

Every autonomous collection operation must execute through a Collection Mission.

Each mission must identify:

- mission ID
- source
- assigned agent
- owning Lane
- schedule or trigger
- priority
- purpose
- collection run
- status
- timestamps
- retries
- outcome
- error history

No hidden collection process may operate outside the mission framework.

---

# Article VII ‚ÄĒ Verification Requirement

Collected information must be evaluated by the Verification Engine before permanent promotion.

Verification may consider:

- source credibility
- source authority
- corroborating sources
- contradictory evidence
- recency
- completeness
- internal consistency
- manipulation indicators
- historical reliability
- verification requirements for the domain

Verification outcomes must remain visible.

Permitted outcomes include:

- confirmed
- partially confirmed
- contradicted
- inconclusive
- rejected
- not required under an approved policy

---

# Article VIII ‚ÄĒ Intelligence DNA‚ĄĘ

Every externally learned fact must carry Intelligence DNA‚ĄĘ.

Intelligence DNA must preserve:

- source provenance
- observation time
- observing agent
- observing Lane
- verification history
- confidence
- relationships
- organizational impact
- usage history
- decisions influenced
- missions influenced
- corrections
- contradictions
- supersession
- retirement

No external intelligence may enter permanent organizational systems without this record.

---

# Article IX ‚ÄĒ Organizational Significance

Not every verified fact becomes an Enterprise Organization Graph node.

Knowledge becomes a graph node only when it has durable organizational significance.

Possible organizational-impact classifications include:

- none
- evidence only
- memory update
- node update
- new node
- mission trigger
- decision support
- risk alert
- executive escalation

Supporting facts should remain attached as evidence or be preserved in Memory Foundry.

This rule exists to prevent node explosion and protect graph quality.

---

---

# Article XI ‚ÄĒ Memory Routing

Verified intelligence that is useful but not graph-significant should be routed to Memory Foundry when appropriate.

Memory routing should preserve:

- context
- source
- confidence
- relationships
- chronology
- organizational relevance
- authorization requirements

Memory Foundry and the Enterprise Organization Graph serve different purposes and must not be treated as interchangeable storage systems.

---

# Article XII ‚ÄĒ Corrections and Contradictions

Corrections, retractions, contradictions, and superseding information must remain historically visible.

The Foundry may update the current organizational understanding, but it may not silently erase the record of how the organization previously understood the information.

Every correction must identify:

- previous value
- corrected value
- reason
- correcting authority
- source
- correction time
- affected nodes
- affected memories
- affected decisions
- affected missions

---

# Article XIII ‚ÄĒ Confidence

Confidence must be explicit.

Confidence scores must be:

- normalized
- explainable
- traceable to evidence
- updated after verification
- revisable after correction
- available to consuming systems

High confidence does not eliminate governance.

Low confidence does not automatically make intelligence useless.

The appropriate action depends on context, risk, and organizational impact.

---

# Article XIV ‚ÄĒ Evidence Preservation

Evidence must remain connected to the intelligence it supports.

Evidence may include:

- official releases
- structured data
- news reports
- regulatory filings
- research papers
- speeches
- market data
- internal records
- documents
- agent observations

Evidence must preserve provenance and licensing classification.

---

# Article XV ‚ÄĒ Executive Escalation

Material intelligence must be escalated to the appropriate Executive Lane.

Examples include:

- security threats ‚Üí Sentinel Lane‚ĄĘ
- technical developments ‚Üí Engineering Lane‚ĄĘ
- economic intelligence ‚Üí Finance Lane‚ĄĘ
- emerging research ‚Üí Research Lane‚ĄĘ
- customer-impacting intelligence ‚Üí Success Lane‚ĄĘ
- material strategic issues ‚Üí Executive Council‚ĄĘ
- founder-critical issues ‚Üí Founder

Escalation must be proportional to materiality and urgency.

---

# Article XVI ‚ÄĒ Human Authority

The Founder and authorized human leaders retain final authority over material organizational decisions.

The Intelligence Foundry may:

- collect
- verify
- organize
- recommend
- alert
- escalate

It may not assume unrestricted executive authority.

---

Required event categories include:

- engine started
- schedule due
- mission created
- mission queued
- mission started
- collection started

- collection failed
- verification started
- verification completed
- verification failed
- DNA created
- DNA updated

# Article XVIII ‚ÄĒ Security and Licensing

Collection may occur only through approved methods.

The Foundry must respect:

- access restrictions
- authentication requirements
- rate limits
- robots and usage policies where applicable
- intellectual-property restrictions
- licensing obligations
- attribution requirements
- privacy requirements
- internal data classifications

Sentinel Lane‚ĄĘ may suspend any source, agent, or collection schedule that presents security or compliance risk.

---

# Article XIX ‚ÄĒ Failure Handling

Collection failures must be visible and isolated.

Failure handling must support:

- error recording
- safe retries
- duplicate prevention
- retry limits
- escalation
- degraded operation
- source suspension
- mission failure classification

Failures may not be silently discarded.

---

# Article XX ‚ÄĒ Source Retirement

Sources may be suspended or retired when they become:

- unreliable
- inactive
- compromised
- legally restricted
- operationally unstable
- redundant
- irrelevant
- excessively costly
- harmful to intelligence quality

Retirement must preserve historical source records and prior Intelligence DNA provenance.

---

# Article XXI ‚ÄĒ Intelligence Quality

The Foundry must optimize for quality rather than volume.

Quality includes:

- relevance
- credibility
- freshness
- completeness
- traceability
- organizational usefulness
- correction resilience
- executive value

Collection volume is not a success metric by itself.

- Finance Lane‚ĄĘ for financial intelligence
- Engineering Lane‚ĄĘ for technical intelligence
